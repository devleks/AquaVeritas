# AquaVeritas: Product Context

## Product Purpose
Real-time freshwater monitoring dashboard powered by on-board satellite inference. The AquaVeritas fine-tuned LFM2.5-VL-450M model runs directly on Sentinel-2 imagery to classify 10 structured fields across water body (core) and agricultural buffer zones. Monitors **20 global freshwater sites** spanning lakes, river deltas, and seasonal wetlands across 6 continents. Analyst-ready prose briefs are generated by Claude Haiku from the structured classification output. Built for the Liquid AI × DPhi Space Hackathon (Hack #05: AI in Space).

## Monitored Sites (20)

16 primary freshwater monitoring targets + 4 saline/hypersaline control sites. Controls have well-documented shrinkage trajectories and dense historical satellite coverage, used to validate model classification against known ground truth.

### Freshwater targets (16)

| Category | Sites |
|---|---|
| Chronic shrinkage | Lake Chad |
| Flooding / extreme seasonal variation | Lake Victoria, Tonle Sap, Okavango Delta |
| Mixed / agricultural signal | Lake Garda (Po Valley), Mekong Delta, Lake Turkana, Lake Titicaca |
| River deltas | Nile Delta, Tana River Delta, Omo River Delta, Amazon Delta, Danube Delta, Mesopotamian Marshes, Niger Delta, Lower Congo River |

### Saline / hypersaline controls (4)

| Site | Type | Why used as control |
|---|---|---|
| Dead Sea | Hypersaline lake | ~1m/yr level drop, the densest documented shrinkage signal globally; shoreline retreat visible in every clear tile |
| Lake Urmia | Hypersaline lake | Lost ~80% volume since 1970s; near-complete transition from open water to salt flat well-quantified |
| Aral Sea South Basin | Formerly freshwater, now hypersaline/dry | Near-total desiccation since 1960s; model should consistently return `dry` / `shrinking`: strong sanity check |
| Salton Sea | Saline lake | Shrinking from reduced agricultural inflow; California water policy makes this a high-interest monitoring site |

## Model Performance

| Metric | Base LFM2.5-VL-450M | Fine-tuned AquaVeritas |
|---|---|---|
| Overall accuracy | ~38% | ~84% |
| Water extent status | ~45% | ~87% |
| Flood risk | ~52% | ~89% |
| Crop stress level | ~28% | ~80% |
| Shoreline encroachment | ~31% | ~82% |

Training: 328 curated Sentinel-2 observations (PASS/MARGINAL), annotated by Claude claude-opus-4-5 oracle. Full fine-tuning on Modal H100 via leap-finetune.

## Register
product; the design serves the monitoring task. Users are in a data-review flow, not a marketing experience.

## Users
- Primary: Hackathon judges evaluating the live system
- Secondary: ML engineers and geospatial researchers monitoring water body health over time

## Brand tone
Precise, mission-critical, understated. The data is the hero. No decorative chrome. Confidence without noise.

## Anti-references
- Flashy SaaS marketing dashboards (no gradients, no hero sections)
- Overcrowded BI tools (no wall-of-widgets)
- Consumer weather apps (no playful illustration)

## Strategic principles
1. Data density where needed, whitespace where not: never force one on the other
2. Color encodes meaning (water status, flood risk, crop stress): never decoration
3. The map is the primary anchor of the interface
4. Live prediction tab must feel fast and trustworthy, not experimental
